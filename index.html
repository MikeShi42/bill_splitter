Overview

The goal of this project is to produce images focused at different depths and with different aperture settings from post-priori from the Stanford Light Field Archive.

Refocusing is done by shifting the images to align at different points and averaging. Changing the aperture is done by averaging less or more images at once.

Summary

I found that light field data was surprisingly straightforward to play with. It'd be interesting for future experiments to actually trace out each individual ray, and try to construct a 3D projection of the scene.
